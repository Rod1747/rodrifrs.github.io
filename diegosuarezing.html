Chilesaurus diegosuarezi

Chilesaurus diegosuarezi is the only known species of the extinct genus Chilesaurus of dinosaur that lived during the Late Jurassic,
150-146 million years ago in what is now South America. Its fossils were discovered in the Aysén region of Chile. At the time of its description,
it was considered one of the few known herbivorous theropods outside of coelurosaurs. It has an unusual combination of theropod, ornithischian,
and sauropodomorph traits. , so this genus has important implications for the understanding of dinosaur evolution, such as the traditional division
between saurischians-ornithischians or as support for the alternative proposal of the Ornithoscelida group. According to the fossils found
in the Toqui Formation, Chilesaurus it coexisted with other small theropods, basal crocodyliforms, and both diplodocid
and titanosaur sauropod dinosaurs.

Description

Chilesaurus measured about 3.2 meters from nose to tail. The small head has small, spatula-shaped
pointing teeth obliquely forward. This dentition, unique to theropods, is typical of a herbivore, indicating
that Chilesaurus fed on plants, a very unusual adaptation in theropods, since most were carnivorous.
